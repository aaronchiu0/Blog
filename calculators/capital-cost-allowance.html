A Bit of Theory

Undepreciated Capital Costs

Calculating UCC is very systematic and thus, is usually done in a spreadsheet software. However, what calculations to do will be explained here.

First of all, we have to understand that UCC Ending of the previous year is the same as the UCC Beginning for the current year. For example, if a company had \$10 000 as their UCC Ending for 2009, then their UCC Beginning will be \$10 000 for 2010.

Your Additions will be what you have purchased and your Dispositions will be what you have sold. Your Adjustment will just be your additions subtracted by your dispositions.

You Base UCC will depend on your adjustment. If the adjustment is positive, you Base UCC will be your UCC Starting plus half of your adjustment. If your adjustment is negative, your Base UCC will simply be your UCC Starting plus your adjustment.

Your CCA will be your Base UCC multiplied by your CCA rate.

Finally, your UCC Ending will be your UCC Beginning plus any additions, subtracted by any dispositions, and subtracted by your CCA. This will become your UCC Beginning for the next year as said before.

Additionally, your Tax Benefit will be your CCA multiplied by the tax rate.
